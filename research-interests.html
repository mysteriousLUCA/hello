Research Interests

Extremophiles in Deep Sea Hydrothermal Vents

This research focuses on the unique microbial communities that thrive in extreme deep-sea environments. By studying these organisms, we gain insights into potential life forms that might exist in similar environments on other celestial bodies.

Our team has collected samples from various hydrothermal vent systems and is analyzing their metabolic pathways and adaptation mechanisms. The findings have implications for both biotechnology applications and astrobiology research.

Keywords:
Extremophiles Hydrothermal Vents Thermophiles Chemosynthesis Astrobiology Microbial Ecology Adaptation Metabolic Pathways

Biosignature Detection Methods for Planetary Exploration

Developing reliable methods to detect signs of life on other planets is crucial for space exploration missions. This research project focuses on creating sensitive and specific techniques that can identify potential biosignatures in extraterrestrial environments.

We are currently working on miniaturized spectroscopy tools and machine learning algorithms that can analyze complex data patterns to distinguish between abiotic and biotic signatures in various planetary conditions.

Keywords:
Biosignatures Spectroscopy Remote Sensing Mars Europa Enceladus Machine Learning Biomarkers Planetary Science

Marine Microbiome and Climate Change

The ocean's microbiome plays a critical role in global biogeochemical cycles and climate regulation. This project investigates how marine microbial communities are responding to climate change and ocean acidification.

Through long-term monitoring and experimental approaches, we are documenting shifts in community composition and function, with particular attention to carbon and nitrogen cycling processes that influence climate feedback mechanisms.

Keywords:
Marine Microbiome Climate Change Ocean Acidification Carbon Cycle Nitrogen Fixation Cyanobacteria Biogeochemistry Ecosystem Services
